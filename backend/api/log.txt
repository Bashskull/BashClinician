To determine if a medication is safe during pregnancy, you should consider the following steps and information:

1. **Consult a Healthcare Professional**: Always consult your doctor or a healthcare professional before taking any medication during pregnancy. They can provide personalized advice based on your specific situation.

2. **FDA Pregnancy Categories**: The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) used to categorize medications based on their safety during pregnancy ( categories A, B, C, D, and X). However, this system has been replaced with more narrative labeling, which includes more detailed information about the potential benefits and risks of using a medication during pregnancy.

3. **Research**: Look up the medication in reliable sources such as the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) or the Organization of Teratology Information Specialists (OTIS) for any known risks during pregnancy.

4. **Consider the Benefits and Risks**: Discuss with your healthcare provider the benefits of using the medication versus the potential risks to the fetus.

5. **Avoid If Possible**: In general, it's best to avoid any unnecessary medication during pregnancy, especially during the first trimester when the fetus's organs are forming.

6. **Common Safe Medications**: Some medications are generally considered safe during pregnancy, such as:
   - Prenatal vitamins
   - Folic acid
   - Some pain relievers like acetaminophen (Tylenol)
   - Certain antibiotics like penicillin
   - Some allergy medications like loratadine (Claritin) and cetirizine (Zyrtec)

7. **Medications to Avoid**: Some medications should be avoided during pregnancy, such as:
   - Ibuprofen (Advil) and other NSAIDs (especially after 20 weeks)
   - Aspirin (in high doses)
   - Warfarin (Coumadin)
   - Isotretinoin (Accutane)
   - Thalidomide
   - Certain anticonvulsants and mood stabilizers
   - Certain antibiotics like tetracycline and doxycycline
